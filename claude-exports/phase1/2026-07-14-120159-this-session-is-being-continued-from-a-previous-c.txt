  A few decisions worth calling out:
  1. Module-level cache: Map<name, Promise> вҖ” SVG fetch + SVGLoader.parse costs
  ~5-15ms per icon, and React 19 will re-render the component tree on every prop
  change. Without a promise cache we'd re-parse on every hover. The _v sentinel
  is a hack to synchronously return the resolved value on subsequent renders вҖ”
  cleaner than Suspense here because I want the whole scene to just fade in
  without a fallback jump.
  2. Y-mirror via negative scale вҖ” SVG's Y-axis points down, Three.js Y points
  up. Mirroring on Y flips the glyph right-side-up in one line instead of
  transforming every path.
  3. Two-layer extrude вҖ” background at 40% depth, glyph at 100%. The glyph sits
  proud so directional light casts a shadow on the background, which is what
  makes it read as embossed rather than a flat sticker.
